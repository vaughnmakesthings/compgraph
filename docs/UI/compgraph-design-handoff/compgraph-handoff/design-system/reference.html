Dark base. Sidebar background (always), dark mode card surfaces, primary body text in light mode. The foundation everything is built on.

Primary accent. CTAs, active nav indicators, focus rings, links, callout borders. The single brand action color across both themes.

Teal Jade
--success · #1B998B → #22C4A8 dark
Success and positive signals. Growth badges, upward deltas, positive callouts. Brightens to #22C4A8 in dark mode for contrast.
Chestnut
--error · #8C2C23 → #D4453A dark
Error, danger, and decline. Destructive buttons, decline badges, risk callouts, downward deltas. Distinct from coral — never confused with primary accent.
Warm Gold
--warning · #DCB256 → #E8C76A dark
Warning and caution. Stale data indicators, low confidence markers, stable/flat trend badges. Sits between coral (attention) and chestnut (danger).

Q4 Competitive Landscape Summary

The fourth quarter saw significant movement in the carrier channel, with BDS Connected Solutions and MarketSource both expanding headcount aggressively. BDS added 89 new roles tied to Samsung Galaxy, concentrated heavily in the Southwest and Southeast regions.

Meanwhile, ActionLink's Microsoft Surface program showed clear signs of contraction, with a 28% reduction in active postings across six markets. Internal language in two postings referenced "program transition," suggesting a possible client loss or restructuring.

Pay rates remained stable industry-wide, with the notable exception of Premium Retail Services, which increased Apple program compensation by an average of $1.50/hr — likely a retention response to MarketSource's aggressive hiring.

Executive Summary

BDS Connected Solutions continues to be the primary competitive threat in the carrier and consumer electronics channel. Their Q4 hiring surge was concentrated in two areas: a 36% expansion of Samsung Galaxy brand ambassador positions, primarily in Dallas, Houston, Phoenix, and Atlanta metro areas, and a quieter but notable entry into the Verizon channel with 24 new assisted selling roles.

Pay rates have softened slightly, dropping 3% quarter-over-quarter to an average of $19.40/hr. This may indicate either margin pressure from clients or a deliberate strategy to hire at higher volume with lower per-head cost. Their Microsoft Surface program continues to shrink, consistent with broader industry trends around Surface retail presence.
